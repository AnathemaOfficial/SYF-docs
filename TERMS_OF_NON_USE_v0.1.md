# Terms of Non-Use

**Canonical — v0.1 (SEALED)**

---

## Purpose

Define **what is explicitly forbidden**, without exceptions or interpretation.

## 1. Absolute Prohibitions

The following uses are **strictly forbidden**:

### Governance & Control

- Human governance (votes, committees, overrides)
- Democratic amendments to the law
- Human-in-the-loop decision making
- Kill-switches or manual intervention

### External Dependencies

- External oracles (time, price, human, consensus)
- Market-based inputs
- External truth sources
- Network-dependent validation

### Behavioral Promises

- Adaptive heuristics or behavioral scoring
- Behavioral promises or outcome guarantees
- Predictive enforcement
- Intent-based authorization

### Financial Mechanisms

- Speculative tokens
- Yield or staking mechanisms
- Financial incentives tied to compliance
- Token-gated access

### Direct Action

- Direct action on reality
- Unmediated physical effects
- Bypassing interposition layer

### Surveillance & Learning

- Intrusive surveillance
- Continuous human monitoring
- Unbounded learning
- Self-modification of invariants

## 2. No Exceptions

No exception is authorized:

- Not temporary
- Not contractual
- Not contextual
- Not emergency-based
- Not jurisdiction-based

## 3. Self-Executing Clause

Any violation immediately triggers:

- Classification as **NON-SYF**
- Automatic revocation of all usage licenses
- Prohibition of any SYF reference

Without notice. Without arbitration. Without appeal.

## 4. Non-Negotiability

These prohibitions:

- Cannot be amended
- Cannot be voted on
- Cannot be suspended
- Cannot be interpreted away
- Cannot be contracted around

## 5. Compliance Verification

Any product claiming SYF compliance must demonstrate:

- Absence of all prohibited mechanisms
- Structural impossibility of violation
- Auditable proof of non-use

## 6. Consequences of Violation

| Violation Type | Immediate Effect |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Governance added | License revoked |
| Oracle dependency | NON-SYF classification |
| Behavioral promise | Reference prohibition |
| Token mechanism | Automatic exclusion |
| Direct action | Full liability transfer |

## Canonical Statement

> *What is forbidden is not debated.*
> *What is debated is not authorized.*
