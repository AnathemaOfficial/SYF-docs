# Boundary of Liability

**Canonical — v0.1 (SEALED)**

---

## Purpose

This document defines a **strict and irrevocable separation of responsibilities** between the law, products, and their usage.

## 1. Fundamental Principle

**Law ≠ Product ≠ Operation**

No liability crosses these boundaries.

## 2. Roles and Responsibilities

**SYF Core Foundation:**

- Defines the law, invariants, and canons.
- Operates no products.
- Has no clients.
- Provides no services.
- Assumes **no operational, commercial, or legal liability**.

**SYFCORP inc.:**

- Designs and distributes products compliant with the law.
- Assumes liability **limited to the product as implementation**.
- Assumes no liability related to final usage, integration, or client environment.

**Client / User:**

- Responsible for integration, configuration, and usage.
- Responsible for hardware, software, and human environment.
- Assumes all liability related to real-world effects.

## 3. No Transitive Liability

No liability:

- flows from product to law,
- flows from usage to Foundation,
- crosses any logical or legal boundary.

## 4. Effect of Non-Compliance

In case of invariant violation:

- Usage license is automatically revoked.
- Product becomes **NON-SYF**.
- All liability remains **limited to product scope**.

## 5. Structural Nature

This separation is:

- Legal
- Technical
- Systemic

It cannot be circumvented by contract, amendment, or interpretation.

## 6. Liability Matrix

| Subject | Foundation | SYFCORP | Client |
|---------|-----------|---------|--------|
| Law definition | ✓ | ✗ | ✗ |
| Product implementation | ✗ | ✓ | ✗ |
| Integration | ✗ | ✗ | ✓ |
| Real-world effects | ✗ | ✗ | ✓ |
| Operational liability | ✗ | Limited | Full |
| Legal liability | ✗ | Product only | Usage only |

## 7. Non-Negotiability Clause

This boundary:

- Cannot be modified by contract
- Cannot be waived by agreement
- Cannot be overridden by jurisdiction
- Cannot be interpreted differently

## Canonical Statement

> *The law is never responsible for its application.*
> *The application is never responsible for its usage.*
