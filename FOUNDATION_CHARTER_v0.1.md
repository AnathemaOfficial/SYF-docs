# SYF Core Foundation — Charter

**Version 0.1 — CANONICAL**

---

## Identity

- **Name**: SYF Core Foundation
- **Nature**: Non-profit entity / Archive
- **Purpose**: Guardian of SYF law and canonical invariants

## Mission

The SYF Core Foundation exists to:

1. **Preserve** the integrity of SYF Core law
2. **Define** canonical invariants
3. **Maintain** sealed documentation
4. **Protect** the mathematical foundation from commercial influence

The Foundation does not build products.
It **defines what cannot be violated**.

## Holdings (Inalienable)

The Foundation holds and protects:

- **SYF Core** — the thermodynamic law
- **FirePlank™** — foundational primitive
- **Proof of Math (PoM)** — validation framework
- **Canonical invariants** (I-1 through I-10)
- **Sealed documents**:
  - ARCHITECTURE_CANON.md
  - INVARIANTS.md
  - TERMS_OF_NON-USE.md
  - BOUNDARY_LAW.md

## Governance Model

**None.**

The Foundation operates without:

- Voting mechanisms
- Committee decisions
- Stakeholder input
- Democratic amendments

The law is **mathematically sealed**, not socially negotiated.

## What the Foundation Does

- Maintains canonical documentation
- Publishes sealed specifications
- Grants conditional usage licenses
- Revokes licenses upon violation
- Preserves historical integrity

## What the Foundation Does NOT Do

- Commercialize
- Sell licenses directly
- Provide services
- Deploy products
- Modify law for third parties
- Accept amendments
- Negotiate exceptions

## Relationship to SYFCORP

The Foundation grants SYFCORP inc. a **conditional, non-exclusive license** to implement SYF Core in commercial products.

This license is:

- Unilateral (Foundation → SYFCORP)
- Conditional (compliance required)
- Revocable (automatic upon violation)
- Non-transferable

**SYFCORP cannot modify, extend, or interpret the law.**

## Canonical Principles

1. **No governance** — law is not voted
2. **No oracle** — no external truth source
3. **No feedback** — no runtime adaptation
4. **No intent** — structure, not purpose
5. **No recovery** — failure is terminal
6. **Machine world only** — no human override

## Immutability Clause

The Foundation may:

- Add new sealed documents
- Extend invariant definitions
- Revoke licenses

The Foundation may NOT:

- Modify existing sealed law
- Create exceptions
- Override invariants
- Negotiate compliance

**What is sealed remains sealed.**

## Canonical Statement

> *The Foundation does not govern.*
> *It preserves what cannot be changed.*
